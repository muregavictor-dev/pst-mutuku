Author · Teacher · Shepherd

The story behind the pages.

Pastor Mutuku did not set out to be an author. For most of his life, his words lived in pulpits, prayer rooms, and quiet conversations after Sunday service — spoken to whoever was in front of him, then gone. It was only after years of people asking, "do you have this written down somewhere," that the books began.

He leads a congregation in Nairobi and has done so for over fourteen years, but his ministry has never stayed inside the walls of a church building. Marriages in crisis, young men with no direction, leaders burning out under the weight of their own platforms — his counsel has followed people into their actual lives, not just their Sunday best.

His Calling

Called to the narrow conversation, not the wide stage.

Pastor Mutuku was raised in a home where faith was practiced quietly and without performance — a posture that has shaped every sermon and chapter he has written since. He describes his calling not as a desire to be heard by many, but a conviction to be obeyed by One.

That conviction took him through years of bivocational ministry — working a day job while pastoring a small group of believers — before the congregation grew into what it is today. He still tells young leaders that the years nobody was watching were the years that actually formed him.

His Mission

To put depth back within reach.

Every book, article, and teaching exists for one purpose: to make serious, costly faith accessible to ordinary believers — not just seminary graduates or full-time ministers. Pastor Mutuku writes the way he preaches, refusing to dilute a hard truth just to make it easier to swallow.

He is intentional about pricing his books within reach of the average Kenyan reader, and about writing in language that speaks to lived experience — work, family, ambition, doubt — rather than abstract theology alone.

A Personal Message

To you, reading this.

If you found your way here, it's probably because something in your faith feels unfinished — a question you haven't said out loud, a calling you keep talking yourself out of, an obedience you've been postponing. I didn't write these books to give you easy answers. I wrote them because I've sat across from too many people carrying that same unfinished feeling, and I know what it costs to keep carrying it alone.

Wherever you are in your walk, I'm glad you're here. Take your time with these pages — and don't be surprised if they ask more of you than they answer for you.

— Pastor Mutuku
